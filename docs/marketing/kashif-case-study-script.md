# Orlixa — Customer Proof Video (Kashif Recruiting) — STATUS + REVISED SCRIPT

## Why this isn't a "results" case-study video (yet)

The original idea was a 60-90s "real company, real results" video. After checking everything the repo
and docs actually record about the Kashif Recruiting tenant, here's what's true:

- **No aggregate result numbers exist anywhere** — no "N candidates processed," no "hours saved," no
  success-rate figure tied to real, ongoing customer usage. The only numbers in the repo are (a) a
  handful of named incident CVs used to debug the AI scoring logic (one was wrongly shortlisted, fixed,
  re-tested — an engineering war-story, not a marketing win), and (b) synthetic seed/test data
  (`scripts/DEMO-CREDENTIALS.md`) explicitly generated by a test script, not organic usage.
- **No real testimonial quote exists.** The three quotes currently on the homepage
  (`Testimonials.tsx` — "James Carter," "Sarah Williams," "David Lee") are fictional placeholder
  copy, not real customers.
- The homepage's hero stats ("300+ Hours Saved," "98.6% Task Success Rate," "500+ Integrations") are
  also hardcoded marketing copy with no data source — not tied to Kashif Recruiting or anyone real.

**Making a video that presents any of the above as "real results" would be the exact opposite of this
project's own honesty-over-mockup rule** — and it's the kind of claim an enterprise prospect (or the
real Kashif Recruiting team, if they ever saw it) could call out. So: don't produce the original brief
as written. Two honest paths forward — pick one, or do both in sequence:

### Option A — "It's real, not a mockup" proof video (producible today, recommended now)
Skip claiming *results*; instead prove the product is genuinely live in production for a real company
— which is itself a strong, honest differentiator most early-stage competitors can't show. Script
below.

### Option B — Ask for a real quote (do this in parallel, takes longer)
Reach out to Kashif Recruiting (the real account owner) and ask for one sentence on how it's going —
even an unpolished WhatsApp-style quote is more powerful than a fabricated one, and it's honest. Once
you have it, this becomes the actual testimonial video, and Option A's b-roll becomes the backdrop for
their real quote.

### Option C — Wait for real numbers
In a few months, once there's genuine candidate/interview volume, revisit this as a real numbers-backed
case study. Don't force it now.

| # | Time | Scene | On-screen text | Visual (real, screen-recorded) |

**Voiceover:**
> This isn't a demo environment — it's a real company, running real work.
> Eleven live workflows automate hiring, onboarding, and HR requests end to end.
> Interviews get scheduled automatically, with a real calendar invite and a real video link.

> Real workflows, for a real company. Yours could be next.

**Important recording note:** get explicit sign-off from Kashif Recruiting before recording or
publishing anything that shows their real company name, email addresses, or real calendar/interview
details — even in a screen-capture, this is their production data. Consider blurring/replacing the
company name and any candidate PII (names, emails, resumes) with placeholders during editing if you
don't get sign-off to show it as-is. If sign-off isn't available, do the same recording on a
throwaway test company configured with the same 11 workflows instead — same honest point ("this is a
real, working product"), zero disclosure risk.
